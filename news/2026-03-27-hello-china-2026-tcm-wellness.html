🔥 The Breaking News

The China Tourism Administration officially unveiled the "Hello! China 2026" initiative in March, marking a significant shift in China's tourism strategy. For the first time, medical tourism—including TCM wellness retreats and modern medical treatment—was explicitly included as a core pillar alongside traditional sightseeing and cultural tourism.

Hainan Province, China's southernmost tropical island, has been designated as the flagship destination for this initiative. The province is investing heavily in infrastructure, hospital accreditation, and international patient services to attract foreign visitors seeking both wellness and medical treatment.

Key components of the initiative include:

  • Simplified visa procedures for medical tourists entering through Hainan's designated ports
  • Subsidies for TCM wellness centers meeting international service standards
  • Expanded use of Boao Lecheng International Medical Tourism Pilot Zone for cutting-edge treatments
  • New international flight routes connecting major Asian cities directly to Haikou and Sanya

🏥 China's Current Landscape: Boao Lecheng Medical Zone

At the heart of Hainan's medical tourism push is the Boao Lecheng International Medical Tourism Pilot Zone (博鳌乐城国际医疗旅游先行区)—China's first and only special zone for medical tourism, established in 2013.

Boao Lecheng offers unique advantages that cannot be found elsewhere in China:

  • Zero-tariff medical devices: Over 200 imported medical devices available duty-free, including advanced imaging equipment and surgical robots
  • Import drug access: Access to internationally approved medications not yet cleared for use in mainland China
  • Streamlined approvals: Faster regulatory approval for new treatments and medical technologies
  • Expert integration: Both renowned TCM masters and internationally trained specialists at partner hospitals

According to official data, Boao Lecheng has served over 50,000 international patients since 2019, with a 40% year-over-year increase in foreign patients in 2025.
