Cancer Treatments Available in China

Chinese hospitals offer the full spectrum of cancer treatments, from conventional therapies to the latest breakthrough innovations:

Surgical Treatments

🔬 Minimally Invasive Surgery

Laparoscopic and robotic-assisted procedures with smaller incisions, less pain, and faster recovery.

🤖 Robotic Surgery

Da Vinci robotic systems for precise tumor removal with minimal damage to surrounding tissue.

⚔️ Complex Tumor Resection

Expertise in removing large or difficult-to-access tumors, including those involving major blood vessels.

Systemic Therapies

💊 Chemotherapy

Conventional and dose-dense regimens tailored to individual cancer types and patient conditions.

🎯 Targeted Therapy

Precision drugs that attack specific cancer mutations with fewer side effects than chemotherapy.

🛡️ Immunotherapy

Checkpoint inhibitors, CAR-T cell therapy, and cancer vaccines to activate the immune system.

🧬 Hormone Therapy

For hormone-sensitive cancers including breast, prostate, and thyroid cancers.

Radiation Therapy

⚡ Proton Therapy

Advanced proton beam therapy that targets tumors with minimal damage to healthy tissue.

🌟 IMRT/VMAT

Intensity-modulated radiation therapy for precise tumor targeting.

🌀 Gamma Knife

Stereotactic radiosurgery for brain tumors and metastases.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does cancer treatment cost in China?

Cancer treatment in China costs 60-80% less than in the US or Europe. For example, chemotherapy costs $3,000-8,000 per cycle in China compared to $15,000-30,000 in the US. A complete cancer surgery package ranges from $10,000-30,000 vs $50,000-150,000 in the US.

Which are the best cancer hospitals in China?

China's top cancer hospitals include: 1) Fudan University Shanghai Cancer Center (#1 in China), 2) Sun Yat-sen University Cancer Center (#2), 3) Chinese Academy of Medical Sciences Cancer Hospital (#3), 4) Peking Union Medical College Hospital, and 5) Tianjin Medical University Cancer Institute. All are ranked among the world's top oncology centers.

Do Chinese doctors speak English?

Top cancer hospitals in China have international departments with English-speaking doctors and staff. We also provide professional medical interpreters to ensure clear communication throughout your treatment.

How long do I need to stay in China for treatment?

Treatment duration varies by case: Initial consultation and testing (3-5 days), surgery (7-14 days hospital stay), chemotherapy (1-2 days per cycle, typically 4-6 cycles), radiation therapy (daily for 4-6 weeks). We help plan your entire stay.

Is the quality of cancer treatment in China comparable to the West?

Yes. Many Chinese cancer hospitals rank among the world's best and treat more patients annually than Western hospitals. Chinese oncologists are highly experienced, and hospitals use the same advanced equipment and therapies available in the US and Europe.

What about follow-up care after I return home?

We provide comprehensive follow-up support including: remote consultations with your Chinese doctor, coordination with your local physician, medication guidance, and monitoring of recovery progress through our patient portal.
