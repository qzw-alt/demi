🧬 Understanding Epilepsy & Treatment Options

Epilepsy is a neurological disorder characterized by recurrent seizures. While medication controls seizures for about 70% of patients, the remaining 30% may require surgical intervention.

Treatment Options:

  • Anti-Epileptic Drugs (AEDs): First-line treatment, 20+ medications available
  • Ketogenic Diet: Specialized diet for drug-resistant epilepsy
  • Vagus Nerve Stimulation (VNS): Implantable device that reduces seizure frequency
  • Responsive Neurostimulation (RNS): Brain-implanted device that monitors and stops seizures
  • Resection Surgery: Remove the seizure focus in the brain
  • Laser Ablation: Minimally invasive procedure to destroy seizure tissue

🔬 Diagnostic Process

  1. Initial Consultation: Review medical history, seizure types, previous treatments
  2. Video-EEG Monitoring: 3-7 days of continuous monitoring to locate seizure focus
  3. Neuroimaging: MRI, PET-CT to identify structural causes
  4. Neuropsychological Testing: Assess cognitive function
  5. Multidisciplinary Conference: Team decides on best treatment approach

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I need surgery?

Surgery is considered when medications fail to control seizures (drug-resistant epilepsy). A comprehensive evaluation including video-EEG and imaging determines if you're a candidate.

How long does the evaluation take?

Video-EEG monitoring typically requires 3-7 days in the hospital. Full surgical evaluation may take 2-3 weeks.

What is the success rate?

For resection surgery, 60-80% of patients achieve significant seizure reduction or freedom. VNS reduces seizures by 50% in about 50% of patients.

Can I get my medications shipped home?

Yes, many hospitals can arrange international shipping of medications or provide prescriptions for home country pharmacies.
