How to Apply for China Medical Visa (M Visa) 2026: Complete Step-by-Step Guide

📅 March 6, 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read 🛂 Visa Guide

Planning medical treatment in China? The first step is obtaining the right visa. China's Medical Visa (M Visa) is specifically designed for foreign patients seeking healthcare services. This comprehensive guide walks you through the entire application process, required documents, fees, and insider tips to ensure a smooth journey.

🎯 What is a China Medical Visa (M Visa)?

The M Visa is a special category of Chinese visa issued to foreigners who need to enter China for medical treatment, diagnosis, or rehabilitation. Unlike tourist visas, the M Visa offers:

  • Longer validity: Up to 180 days per stay
  • Multiple entries: Option for multiple entries if follow-up visits are needed
  • Extension possibility: Can be extended within China if treatment requires more time
  • Accompanying family: Family members can apply for S2 visa to accompany patient

ℹ️ Important Note

The M Visa is not the same as a tourist (L) or business (M) visa. Attempting to enter China for medical treatment on the wrong visa type can result in entry denial or complications with hospital admission.

📋 Step-by-Step Application Process

1

Choose Your Hospital and Get an Invitation Letter

Before applying for a visa, you must have a confirmed appointment or treatment plan from a Chinese hospital. Most major hospitals with international departments can issue official invitation letters for visa purposes.

What the hospital should provide:

  • Official invitation letter on hospital letterhead
  • Treatment plan or estimated duration of stay
  • Hospital registration certificate (复印件)
  • Doctor's contact information for verification

Tip: Contact the hospital's International Patient Services department 4–6 weeks before your planned travel date to allow time for document preparation.

2

Gather Required Documents

Prepare the following documents for your visa application:

Required Documents:

  • Valid passport: Must have at least 6 months validity and blank visa pages
  • Visa application form (Form V.2013): Completed and signed
  • Recent passport photo: 33mm x 48mm, white background, taken within 6 months
  • Hospital invitation letter: Original or scanned copy
  • Medical records: Diagnosis, test results, current treatment summary (English or Chinese)
  • Proof of financial means: Bank statements, insurance coverage letter, or hospital prepayment receipt
  • Flight itinerary: Round-trip booking or detailed travel plan
  • Accommodation proof: Hotel booking or hospital patient housing confirmation
3

Complete the Online Application

China now requires all visa applicants to complete an online application form before submitting documents in person.

  1. Visit the China Online Visa Application (COVA) system
  2. Create an account and complete Form V.2013
  3. Select "Medical" as the purpose of visit
  4. Upload your passport photo digitally
  5. Print and sign the completed application form

⚠️ Common Mistake

Make sure your passport photo meets exact specifications. Many applications are delayed due to photos that are too dark, have shadows, or incorrect dimensions.

4

Submit Application at Chinese Embassy/Consulate

Submit your application in person or through an authorized visa agency:

Submission options:

  • In person: At the Chinese embassy or consulate in your country
  • Visa agency: Authorized third-party service (recommended for first-time applicants)
  • CVASC: China Visa Application Service Centers (in select countries)

Processing time: Standard processing takes 4–5 business days. Express service (2–3 days) may be available for an additional fee.

5

Pay Visa Fees and Collect Passport

Visa fees vary by nationality, number of entries, and processing speed:

Type Standard Fee Express Fee
Single Entry (30 days) $140 $170
Double Entry $140 $170
Multiple Entry (6 months) $140 $170
Multiple Entry (12 months) $140 $170

Note: Fees vary by country. US citizens typically pay $185 for all visa types. Check with your local embassy for exact fees.

⏱️ Typical Processing Timeline

Week 1: Hospital Contact & Documentation

Contact hospital, receive invitation letter, gather medical records

Week 2: Online Application

Complete COVA form, prepare supporting documents, take passport photo

Week 3: Submission & Processing

Submit application, pay fees, wait for processing (4–5 business days)

Week 4: Final Preparations

Collect passport with visa, book flights, confirm accommodation

✅ Pro Tip: Start Early

Begin the visa application process at least 6–8 weeks before your planned treatment date. This allows buffer time for document delays, additional medical tests, or visa processing issues.

🎒 For Accompanying Family Members

If family members need to accompany you during treatment, they should apply for an S2 Visa (Short-term Private Affairs Visa):

S2 Visa Requirements:

  • Valid passport with 6+ months validity
  • Completed visa application form
  • Passport photo
  • Invitation letter from the patient (explaining relationship and need for accompaniment)
  • Proof of relationship: Marriage certificate, birth certificate, etc.
  • Patient's passport and visa copy
  • Hospital confirmation letter mentioning family accommodation

💡 Insider Tips for a Smooth Application

📝 Document Translation

All medical records should be in English or Chinese. If translation is needed, use certified translation services—embassies may reject unofficial translations.

💰 Financial Proof

Show proof of funds covering treatment costs + $100/day for living expenses. Hospital prepayment receipts are highly effective.

📞 Direct Communication

Provide the hospital's direct contact number on your application. Some embassies verify invitations by calling the hospital.

🔄 Visa Extensions

If treatment takes longer than expected, you can extend your M Visa within China at the local Exit & Entry Administration. Apply 7 days before expiry.

🏥 Hospital Selection

Choose hospitals with established International Patient Services. They're experienced with visa documentation and can provide comprehensive support.

✈️ Travel Insurance

Purchase travel insurance that covers medical evacuation. Some embassies require proof of insurance for medical visa applicants.

❌ Common Reasons for Visa Rejection

Avoid these common pitfalls that can delay or derail your application:

⚠️ Watch Out For These Issues

  • Incomplete hospital documentation: Invitation letter missing hospital seal or doctor's signature
  • Insufficient financial proof: Bank statements showing inconsistent balances or recent large deposits without explanation
  • Passport validity issues: Less than 6 months validity or damaged passport pages
  • Photo problems: Wrong dimensions, poor lighting, or photos older than 6 months
  • Inconsistent information: Discrepancies between application form and supporting documents
  • Previous visa violations: Overstaying previous Chinese visas can result in application denial

🆕 2025-2026 Policy Updates

📢 New COVA System (September 2025)

China launched a new China Online Visa Application System (COVA) in September 2025. Key changes include:

  • Streamlined online form with auto-save functionality
  • Digital photo upload with real-time validation
  • Application tracking through the system
  • Reduced paperwork for certain visa categories

🎉 Good News: Fingerprint Exemption

From December 18, 2025 to December 31, 2026, fingerprint collection is exempted for all short-term visa applicants (staying for no longer than 180 days). This speeds up the application process significantly.
