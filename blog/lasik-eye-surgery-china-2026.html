🔬 Understanding Laser Vision Correction Technologies

SMILE (Small Incision Lenticule Extraction)

  • How it works: A femtosecond laser creates a thin lenticule inside the cornea, removed through a small 2-4mm incision
  • Advantages: No flap creation, less dry eye, faster recovery, suitable for active lifestyles
  • Best for: Myopia up to -10.00D, patients with dry eye concerns, athletes

Femtosecond LASIK

  • How it works: Femtosecond laser creates a corneal flap, excimer laser reshapes cornea underneath
  • Advantages: Fast visual recovery (24 hours), treats wider range of prescriptions
  • Best for: Myopia up to -12.00D, hyperopia, astigmatism

SmartSurfACE PRK (TranPRK)

  • How it works: Surface ablation without flap creation, epithelium removed by laser
  • Advantages: No flap complications, preserves corneal strength
  • Best for: Thin corneas, patients with high risk of eye trauma

ICL (Implantable Collamer Lens)

  • How it works: A biocompatible lens implanted inside the eye, behind the iris
  • Advantages: Reversible, preserves corneal tissue, treats very high myopia
  • Best for: High myopia (-6.00D to -20.00D), thin corneas, dry eyes

✅ How to Choose the Right Eye Hospital

5 Key Criteria for Selection

  1. Hospital Qualifications: Choose Grade 3A hospitals or certified specialized eye hospitals with refractive surgery licenses
  2. Equipment: Look for Zeiss VisuMax femtosecond lasers, Schwind Amaris excimer lasers, or equivalent top-tier devices
  3. Surgeon Experience: Minimum 5 years experience, 5,000+ successful cases, relevant certifications
  4. Preoperative Examination: Should include 18+ tests, taking at least 1 hour to ensure surgical candidacy
  5. Postoperative Service: Lifetime follow-up, 24-hour emergency response, vision regression policies

📝 Preparing for Your Vision Correction Journey

Before Surgery:

  • Stop wearing contact lenses: Soft lenses (1 week), RGP (2 weeks), Hard lenses (3-4 weeks)
  • Age requirement: 18-50 years old
  • Stable prescription: No significant change in past 2 years
  • No active eye diseases or severe dry eye
  • No pregnancy or breastfeeding

What to Expect:

  • Consultation: Comprehensive eye exam (1-2 hours)
  • Surgery day: Procedure takes 10-15 minutes per eye, no hospitalization needed
  • Recovery:
    • SMILE/LASIK: Clear vision within 24 hours, return to normal activities in 2-3 days
    • PRK: 3-5 days for epithelial healing, full clarity in 1-2 weeks
  • Follow-up: 1 day, 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, then annually

Postoperative Care:

  • No eye rubbing for 1 month
  • No swimming or hot tubs for 1 month
  • No eye makeup for 2 weeks
  • Use prescribed eye drops as directed
  • Wear protective eyewear during sleep for first week
