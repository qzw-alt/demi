What's Included in the Cost?

When you receive a treatment quote from Chinese hospitals, it typically includes:

  • Initial Consultation: $50-150 (vs $300-500 in US)
  • Diagnostic Tests: CT, MRI, PET-CT, biopsies
  • Treatment: Surgery, chemotherapy, radiation, or targeted therapy
  • Hospital Stay: Private rooms available
  • Post-Treatment Care: Follow-up consultations
  • Medical Translation: English-speaking coordinators

Not included: Travel, accommodation, meals, and companion expenses.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the quality of cancer care in China comparable to Western countries?

Yes. Top Chinese cancer hospitals are JCI accredited and many are ranked among the world's best. Chinese oncologists publish extensively in international journals and participate in global clinical trials.

How long do I need to stay in China for treatment?

Duration varies by treatment type: Surgery typically requires 2-3 weeks; chemotherapy cycles may span 3-6 months with periodic visits; radiation therapy usually requires 4-6 weeks of daily treatment.

Will my insurance cover treatment in China?

Many international insurance plans cover overseas treatment. We can provide all necessary documentation for insurance claims. Some patients also use medical tourism insurance.

Is language a barrier?

No. Major cancer hospitals have dedicated international departments with English-speaking staff. Medical translators are available for consultations and throughout your treatment.
