Complete step-by-step application process for medical tourists

📌 Quick Summary: Most medical tourists need an M Visa (Business Visa) or Q2 Visa (Family Visit) for medical treatment in China. Processing time is typically 4-7 working days, and you'll need an invitation letter from your hospital.

🎯 Which Visa Do You Need?

Visa Type Best For Duration Invitation Letter
M Visa (Business) Medical treatment, consultations 30-90 days ✅ Required
Q2 Visa (Family) Visiting family + treatment 30-180 days ❌ Not required
L Visa (Tourist) Tourism + minor treatment 30 days ❌ Not required
144-hour Transit Emergency/Short consultations 6 days ❌ Not required
⚠️ Important: While L (tourist) visa allows some medical activities, we strongly recommend the M visa for serious medical treatment. It's more appropriate, allows longer stays, and shows clear intent to immigration officers.

📋 Required Documents Checklist

For M Visa (Medical Business Visa):

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity, 2+ blank pages)
  • Completed visa application form (Form V.2013)
  • Recent passport photo (48mm x 33mm, white background)
  • Hospital invitation letter (we provide this)
  • Medical records summary (English or Chinese)
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or hospital letter)
  • Flight itinerary (round-trip recommended)
  • Visa fee payment ($140-200 depending on nationality)

📝 Step-by-Step Application Process

1Choose Your Hospital

Select the hospital and doctor for your treatment. We can help you find the best match based on your condition.

2Get Hospital Invitation Letter

The hospital will issue an official invitation letter confirming your appointment. This is the most important document for your M visa application.

💡 Our Service: We coordinate with hospitals to get your invitation letter within 3-5 working days. This is included in our Standard ($299) and Premium packages.
3Fill Online Application

Complete the visa application form at visaforchina.cn or your local Chinese embassy website.

Key fields to complete:

  • Purpose of visit: "Business" (M visa) or "Medical Treatment"
  • Intended date of entry: Plan 1-2 weeks before your appointment
  • Duration of stay: Request enough time for treatment + recovery
4Book Appointment at Visa Center

Schedule an appointment at your nearest Chinese Visa Application Service Center (CVASC). Available in major cities worldwide.

5Submit Documents in Person

Attend your appointment with all required documents. Biometric data (fingerprints) will be collected.

6Collect Your Visa

Processing typically takes 4-7 working days. Express service (2-3 days) available for extra fee.

💡 Pro Tips for Success

✅ Do:
  • Apply at least 1 month before your planned travel date
  • Ensure your passport has 6+ months validity
  • Get your hospital invitation letter first (we help with this)
  • Book refundable flights until visa is approved
  • Bring medical records to support your application
❌ Don't:
  • Apply for a tourist visa if you're coming specifically for surgery
  • Wait until the last minute to apply
  • Forget to mention your medical condition if asked
  • Overstay your visa (can result in fines and future entry bans)

🌍 144-Hour Visa-Free Transit Option

For short consultations or follow-ups, you may qualify for the 144-hour (6-day) visa-free transit policy available in:

  • Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Chengdu, and 14 other cities
  • Must arrive from and depart to different countries
  • Cannot leave the designated area
  • Perfect for: Second opinions, consultations, quick check-ups

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I extend my medical visa while in China?

Yes, M visas can typically be extended once for medical reasons. You'll need a letter from your hospital confirming ongoing treatment. Apply at the local Exit & Entry Administration 7-10 days before your current visa expires.

What if my visa application is rejected?

Rejection is rare if you have proper documentation. Common reasons: incomplete documents, unclear purpose, or passport issues. If rejected, you can reapply with additional supporting documents. We can help strengthen your application with a detailed hospital letter.

Can my family member accompany me?

Yes, family members can apply for Q2 (family visit) visas or accompany you on the same M visa if they're also receiving treatment. Each person needs their own application.

Do I need travel insurance?

While not required for the visa, we strongly recommend comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical treatment abroad. Many hospitals also require proof of payment ability.
