China Medical Visa Guide 2026: S2 Visa Application Step-by-Step

Published: March 5, 2026 | Reading time: 12 minutes

Planning medical treatment in China? Understanding the visa requirements is your first critical step. This comprehensive guide covers everything international patients need to know about China medical visas in 2026, including the popular S2 visa, application procedures, required documents, and expert tips to ensure a smooth approval process.

Understanding China Medical Visa Types

China offers specific visa categories for medical travelers. Choosing the correct visa type is essential—applying for the wrong category can result in rejection, entry denial, or treatment delays.

S2 Visa: Short-Term Medical Treatment (Most Common)

The S2 visa is the primary choice for most international patients seeking treatment in China. This visa category covers medical stays up to 180 days and accommodates various treatment types including surgeries, rehabilitation, specialist consultations, and follow-up care.

Key features of the S2 visa:

  • Valid for single, double, or multiple entries
  • Maximum stay: 180 days per visit
  • Extensions possible: Two 90-day extensions allowed within China
  • Family accompaniment: Spouse, parents, children, grandparents, and siblings can apply
  • Processing time: 4-7 business days (standard), 2-3 days (express)

S1 Visa: Long-Term Medical Treatment

For patients requiring extended treatment exceeding 180 days, the S1 visa is the appropriate choice. This category covers complex treatments such as cancer therapy, organ transplants, and extended rehabilitation programs.

Important: S1 visa holders must convert to a residence permit within 30 days of arrival. These permits can remain valid for up to 5 years, depending on treatment duration.

L Visa: Tourist Visa Limitations

⚠️ Important Warning

Do not use an L (tourist) visa for planned medical treatment. While some patients attempt this for minor consultations, hospitals may refuse inpatient procedures for L visa holders. Border officials can deny entry if they discover medical intent inconsistent with tourist purposes. Always apply for the appropriate S2 visa for planned treatments.

Required Documents for S2 Medical Visa

Preparing complete documentation is crucial for visa approval. Missing or incorrect documents are the leading cause of application delays.

Basic Documents (All Applicants)

  • Valid Passport: Must have at least 6 months validity and 2 blank visa pages
  • Visa Application Form: Completed online via the COVA system (https://consular.mfa.gov.cn/VISA/)
  • Recent Passport Photo: 33mm x 48mm, white background, taken within 6 months
  • Proof of Residence: Driver's license, utility bill, or bank statement showing your current address
  • Visa Application Statement: Download from embassy website, handwritten signature required

Medical-Specific Documents

  • Hospital Invitation Letter: Official letter from a Grade A Level 3 hospital in China confirming your treatment plan, expected duration, and medical necessity
  • Medical Records: Relevant diagnosis, test results, and treatment history from your home country (translated to Chinese or English)
  • Financial Proof: Bank statements showing sufficient funds to cover treatment and living expenses (minimum $10,000 USD recommended)
  • Health Insurance: Proof of international health insurance covering treatment in China (if available)

💡 Pro Tip: Hospital Invitation Letter

The hospital invitation letter is the most critical document. Contact your chosen hospital's International Department at least 2-3 weeks before your visa application. Provide them with your medical records and they will issue an official invitation letter with hospital stamp and doctor's signature.

Step-by-Step S2 Visa Application Process

Step 1: Contact Your Hospital (2-3 weeks before)

Reach out to your chosen hospital's International Department. Send your medical records and request a treatment plan. Once they confirm they can treat you, they will issue an invitation letter. This process typically takes 1-2 weeks.

Step 2: Complete Online Application (COVA System)

Visit the China Online Visa Application System at https://consular.mfa.gov.cn/VISA/. Create an account and fill out the application form carefully. Select "S2" as your visa type and "medical treatment" as the purpose.

Important tips for the online form:

  • Enter information exactly as it appears in your passport
  • Provide detailed but accurate information about your medical condition
  • Upload all required documents in the specified formats (PDF, JPG)
  • Double-check all entries before submission—errors can cause delays

Step 3: Schedule Appointment and Submit Documents

After your online application shows "Passport to be submitted" status, schedule an appointment at the Chinese Embassy or Consulate in your jurisdiction. Bring:

  • Original passport
  • Printed application form with barcode
  • Original hospital invitation letter
  • All supporting documents (originals and copies)
  • Visa fee payment (cash or money order, varies by country)

Step 4: Visa Processing and Collection

Standard processing takes 4-7 business days. Express service (2-3 days) is available for an additional fee. You will receive a pickup slip with the collection date. Bring this slip to collect your passport with visa.

Visa Fees and Processing Times (2026)

Visa fees vary by nationality and processing speed. Here are general guidelines for US applicants:

  • Standard Processing (4-7 days): $140 USD
  • Express Processing (2-3 days): $160 USD
  • Rush Processing (1 day): $180 USD

Some nationalities qualify for different fee structures. Check with your local Chinese embassy for exact amounts.

Common Application Mistakes to Avoid

Based on our experience helping hundreds of international patients, these are the most common pitfalls:

Mistake 1: Insufficient Financial Documentation

Many applicants underestimate the financial proof required. Chinese authorities want assurance you can cover treatment costs without burdening local resources. Provide bank statements showing at least 120% of your estimated treatment costs.

Mistake 2: Vague Medical Documentation

General statements like "need medical treatment" are insufficient. Your hospital invitation letter should specify the diagnosis, proposed treatment, expected duration, and estimated costs.

Mistake 3: Wrong Visa Category

Applying for an M (business) or L (tourist) visa for medical purposes is a common error. These visas are not appropriate for planned medical treatment and can result in denial or deportation.

Mistake 4: Last-Minute Applications

Visa processing takes time, and delays can happen. Apply at least 4-6 weeks before your planned travel date to allow for any complications or additional document requests.

Visa Extensions and Multiple Entries

If your treatment requires more time than initially planned, S2 visa holders can apply for extensions within China:

  • Apply at the local Exit and Entry Administration Bureau
  • Submit updated hospital documentation showing continued treatment need
  • Two 90-day extensions are typically allowed
  • Process takes 7-10 business days

For patients requiring multiple trips (follow-up treatments, rehabilitation phases), apply for a multiple-entry S2 visa initially. This saves time and money compared to applying for new visas each visit.

Family Accompaniment

Medical travel can be stressful, and having family support is invaluable. S2 visas allow family members to accompany patients:

  • Eligible family members: Spouse, parents, children, grandparents, siblings
  • Each family member needs a separate S2 visa application
  • Documentation: Marriage certificates, birth certificates, or other proof of relationship (translated and notarized)
  • Hospital letter: Should mention accompanying family members and their relationship to the patient

After Arrival in China

Once you arrive in China with your medical visa:

  • Registration: Register with local police within 24 hours (your hospital can assist)
  • Hospital Check-in: Present your passport and visa at the International Department
  • Keep Documents Safe: Maintain copies of your visa, passport, and hospital documents
  • Track Visa Expiry: Set reminders for visa expiration and extension deadlines

Conclusion

Securing the right medical visa is the foundation of a successful treatment journey in China. The S2 visa serves most patients' needs, offering flexibility for various treatment durations and the possibility of extensions if needed.

Start your visa application process early, gather complete documentation, and work closely with your chosen hospital's International Department. With proper preparation, your visa application should proceed smoothly, allowing you to focus on what matters most—your health and recovery.

China's world-class healthcare system welcomes international patients, and the visa process, while detailed, is straightforward when approached systematically. Your journey to better health in China begins with that first step—the right visa.
