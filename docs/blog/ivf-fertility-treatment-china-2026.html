🔬 Understanding IVF Technologies

Conventional IVF

Eggs and sperm are combined in laboratory dish for fertilization. Best for: tubal factor infertility, unexplained infertility, endometriosis.

ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection)

Single sperm is injected directly into egg. Best for: male factor infertility, previous fertilization failure, low sperm count or motility.

PGT (Preimplantation Genetic Testing)

Embryos are tested for genetic abnormalities before transfer. Types include:

  • PGT-A: Aneuploidy screening for chromosomal abnormalities
  • PGT-M: Testing for monogenic/single gene disorders
  • PGT-SR: Structural rearrangement screening

Fertility Preservation

Egg freezing, sperm freezing, and embryo freezing for future use. Increasingly popular among career-focused women and cancer patients.

📋 The IVF Process: Step by Step

  1. Initial Consultation: Medical history review, fertility testing, treatment plan discussion
  2. Ovarian Stimulation: 10-14 days of hormone injections to produce multiple eggs
  3. Monitoring: Regular ultrasounds and blood tests to track follicle development
  4. Trigger Shot: Final injection to mature eggs for retrieval
  5. Egg Retrieval: Minor surgical procedure under sedation (15-30 minutes)
  6. Fertilization: Eggs fertilized with sperm in laboratory
  7. Embryo Culture: Embryos grown for 3-6 days
  8. Embryo Transfer: Selected embryo(s) transferred to uterus
  9. Pregnancy Test: Blood test 10-14 days after transfer

✅ Factors Affecting IVF Success

Key Success Factors

  • Age: Most critical factor. Women under 35 have 60-70% success; over 40 drops to 20-30%
  • Ovarian Reserve: Quantity and quality of remaining eggs
  • Sperm Quality: Count, motility, and morphology
  • Uterine Health: Endometrial thickness and receptivity
  • Embryo Quality: Genetic normalcy and developmental potential
  • Lifestyle: Weight, smoking, alcohol, stress levels
