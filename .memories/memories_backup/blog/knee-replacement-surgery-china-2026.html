The Process: What to Expect

Before You Arrive (2-4 weeks)

  1. Send your medical records and X-rays/MRI for review
  2. Receive detailed treatment plan and cost estimate
  3. Apply for medical visa (hospital provides invitation letter)
  4. Book flights and accommodation (hospital can assist)

Upon Arrival (Day 1-2)

  1. Airport pickup by hospital coordinator
  2. Comprehensive pre-operative assessment
  3. Meet your surgeon for final consultation
  4. Pre-surgery preparation and tests

Surgery and Recovery (Day 3-10)

  1. Surgery (typically 1-2 hours)
  2. 2-3 days in hospital for monitoring
  3. Daily physiotherapy begins
  4. Walking with assistance by day 2-3
  5. Discharge with full instructions

Post-Discharge (Week 2-3)

  1. Stay in local hotel for follow-up appointments
  2. Daily outpatient physiotherapy
  3. Stitch removal (usually day 12-14)
  4. Final check-up and clearance to fly home

Risks and How to Mitigate Them

Risk 1: Language Barriers

Mitigation: Choose hospitals with dedicated international departments. All five hospitals listed have English-speaking coordinators who stay with you throughout.

Risk 2: Follow-up Care

Mitigation: Get your local doctor involved before you travel. Share all records. Schedule post-return check-ups in advance.

Risk 3: Complications

Mitigation: Knee replacement complication rates are 1-2% globally. Chinese top-tier hospitals match these statistics. Ensure you have travel insurance covering medical complications.

Risk 4: Cultural Differences

Mitigation: Medical tourism facilitators handle logistics. Focus on recovery while they manage the details.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I need to stay in China?

Plan for 2-3 weeks. Surgery on day 2-3, hospital stay 5-7 days, then 7-10 days of outpatient physiotherapy and follow-ups before flying home.

Will my insurance cover it?

Some international insurers cover overseas treatment. Check with your provider. Many patients use the savings to pay out-of-pocket and still come out ahead.

What about the implants? Are they safe?

Top hospitals use FDA-approved implants from Zimmer, Stryker, DePuy - the same brands used in Western hospitals. You can request specific brands.

Can I get both knees done at once?

Yes, bilateral knee replacement is common. Recovery is slightly longer, but you only make one trip. Most patients choose this option.

What if something goes wrong after I return home?

Complications are rare, but hospitals provide 24/7 remote consultation. Your local orthopedic surgeon can handle most issues using the detailed discharge records provided.
