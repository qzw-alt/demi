Gastric Sleeve (Sleeve Gastrectomy)

The most common weight loss procedure worldwide. The surgeon removes approximately 75-80% of the stomach, leaving a sleeve-shaped stomach about the size of a banana. It limits food intake and reduces hunger hormones.

Expected weight loss: 60-80% of excess body weight within 18-24 months

Best for: Patients with BMI 35-50 who haven't had previous stomach surgery

Gastric Bypass (Roux-en-Y)

Creates a small stomach pouch and reroutes the intestines, reducing both food intake and calorie absorption. More complex than sleeve gastrectomy but generally produces slightly greater long-term weight loss.

Expected weight loss: 70-85% of excess body weight within 2 years

Best for: Patients with BMI 40+ or those with obesity-related conditions like type 2 diabetes

Gastric Balloon (Intragastric Balloon)

A non-surgical option where a silicone balloon is placed in the stomach via endoscopy (no incision required). Filled with saline to create a feeling of fullness. Temporary — removed after 6-12 months.

Expected weight loss: 20-35% of excess body weight while the balloon is in place

Best for: Patients with BMI 27-35 who want a non-surgical option or need to lose weight before a more invasive procedure

LAP-BAND (Adjustable Gastric Band)

An adjustable silicone band placed around the upper stomach, creating a small pouch. The band's tightness can be adjusted without further surgery. Fully reversible.

Expected weight loss: 40-55% of excess body weight over 3-5 years

Best for: Patients who want a reversible option or are not candidates for more invasive procedures

✅ Am I a Candidate for Weight Loss Surgery?

General Criteria for Bariatric Surgery

  • BMI 35 or higher — Severe obesity, no other conditions required
  • BMI 30-35 — With at least one obesity-related condition (type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, sleep apnea, joint pain)
  • Age 18-65 — Some hospitals accept patients outside this range on a case-by-case basis
  • Previous weight loss attempts — Documented attempts at diet and exercise programs
  • No active substance abuse
  • Realistic expectations — Understanding of the lifestyle changes required post-surgery

⚠️ Risks, Recovery & What to Expect

Common Post-Surgery Experiences

  • First 2 weeks: Liquid diet only. Fatigue is common. Some patients experience nausea as stomach adjusts.
  • Month 1-2: Transition to soft/pureed foods. Rapid initial weight loss begins.
  • Month 3-6: Solid foods gradually reintroduced. Average 30-40% of target weight loss achieved.
  • Month 6-18: Continued weight loss. Hair thinning sometimes occurs (temporary). Skin sagging may require body contouring surgery.
  • Year 2+: Weight stabilizes. Long-term vitamin and protein supplementation usually necessary.

Potential Risks

Like any surgery, bariatric procedures carry risks. At JCI-accredited Chinese hospitals, complication rates are comparable to Western standards:

  • Short-term complications: Infection (1-2%), bleeding (1-2%), staple line leak (less than 1%)
  • Long-term considerations: Nutritional deficiencies, dumping syndrome, gallstones, excess skin
  • Rare complications: Blood clots (0.3-1%), pulmonary embolism (0.3%)

Most complications are manageable with prompt medical attention, which is why choosing a hospital with proper post-operative monitoring is critical.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is weight loss surgery safe in China?

Yes — at JCI-accredited hospitals with experienced bariatric surgeons, complication rates are comparable to leading international centers. The key is choosing a hospital with a dedicated weight management program and board-certified bariatric surgeons.

Will my insurance cover surgery in China?

Most international insurance plans do not cover elective surgery abroad. However, some international health insurers now offer medical tourism riders. Check with your provider. Self-pay is still significantly cheaper even at full cost.

Do I need a medical visa to travel to China for surgery?

Most nationalities need a visa. We can provide a medical invitation letter from the hospital to support your visa application. M-category visas typically allow stays of 30-60 days — sufficient for the procedure and initial recovery.

How do I communicate with medical staff if I don't speak Chinese?

All hospitals on our platform have dedicated international departments with English-speaking coordinators, translators, and medical staff. Many have coordinators who speak Arabic, Spanish, French, and Russian as well.

What happens if I have complications after leaving China?

We coordinate with your local doctor for post-operative follow-up. Most common complications (if they occur) appear within the first 2 weeks, so we require patients to remain in China or nearby for at least 14 days post-surgery before long-distance travel.

How much weight will I actually lose?

Results vary by procedure and individual. Gastric sleeve patients typically lose 60-80% of their excess weight within 18-24 months. Most patients lose 20-40 kg (44-88 lbs) in the first year. Success depends heavily on adherence to dietary guidelines and follow-up appointments.

Can I get gastric sleeve and tummy tuck (body contouring) at the same time?

These are typically staged separately. Most surgeons recommend waiting 12-18 months after bariatric surgery before body contouring, once weight has stabilized. Some hospitals offer staged procedures at reduced combined costs.
